PIECES:

There are sixteen pieces in a chess game. They are:

  • KING
  • QUEEN
  • ROOK
  • BISHOP
  • KNIGHT
  • PAWN

KING:

The king is the MOST IMPORTANT PIECE in the whole game. If you were to lose it, you'd lose the game. When your king is under attack (being under the line of attack), it's called CHECK and you are forced to reply by either:

  • Capturing the attacking piece
  • Blocking the check
  • Moving the king

The king is one of the least mobile pieces in the game, being able to move in all directions, but only one square at a time. It cannot move into a square controlled by an opponent's piece.

When none of this is possible, it is called CHECKMATE and it's game over.

QUEEN:

The queen is said to be one of the most powerful piece, and for good reason, as it move just like the king, but can move as many squares as it likes. It only has one restriction, that being, it cannot pass through any piece or jump over it. It is restricted by it's teammates, and if there is an opponent piece in it's line of sight, it can capture it, i.e. remove the piece and land on it's square.

ROOK:

The rook is like the queen, however, it can not move diagonally. It can only move on ranks(rows) and files(columns). You get 2 of them

BISHOP:

The bishop can only move diagonally. It has the same restrictions as the queen. You also get 2 of them.

KNIGHT:

The knight is a special piece. It is the only piece capable of jumping over other pieces. It moves very weirdly, moving in an L shape (2 squares forward, 1 square side). It cannot capture pieces it jumps over, but rather captures the piece which is on the square it lands on. You also get 2 of them.

PAWN:

The pawn is another special piece. It has the least mobility, moving only one square forward, except on it's starting square, on which it has the option of moving two squares instead of 1.

It can only capture that is diagonally in front of it. You get 8 of them, in front of all your pieces.

UNUSUAL MOVES:

In chess, there are three unusual moves. They are:

  • Castling:

  • En Passant:

    • If an opponent pawn is at it's beginning stage, and it moves 2 squares, adjacent to your pawn on the same rank, the pawn can en passant, meaning it can capture the pawn as if it had moved 1 step.

  • Pawn Promotion:

    • When the pawn reaches the topmost rank, it has the option to turn into a queen, rook, bishop or knight.

    Bishop = 3
    Knight = 3
